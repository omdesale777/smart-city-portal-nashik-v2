Ram Mandir ⭐ 4.8
Temple · Shrine
Kalaram Temple
श्री काळाराम मंदिर

Built in 1792, this iconic black-stone temple houses Lord Rama's black idol (Kalaram). Scene of Dr. Ambedkar's historic Satyagraha in 1930.

📍 Panchavati, 3 km 🕐 5:30AM – 10PM 🅿️ Free Entry

Holy Ghat ⭐ 4.7
Holy Ghat · Kumbh Mela Venue
Ramkund
रामकुंड · पंचवटी

The holiest ghat in Nashik, on the banks of the Godavari. Believed to be where Lord Rama bathed during his exile. Contains the sacred Asthivilaya Kund where ashes of Nehru, Indira Gandhi, and countless others have been immersed. The primary venue of the Simhastha Kumbh Mela held every 12 years — one of the largest human gatherings on Earth.

📍 Panchavati, 2 km from CBS 🕐 Open all day 🅿️ Free Entry 🪔 Kumbh Mela Venue

Calendar of Celebrations

Spiritual Events
& Festivals

🪔
Every 12 Years
Simhastha Kumbh Mela
The largest religious gathering on Earth, held on the banks of Godavari at Ramkund. Next: 2027. Millions of pilgrims take a holy dip during Shahi Snan.
🌸
March – April
Ram Navami
The birthday of Lord Rama celebrated with grand processions (Rath Yatra) from Kalaram Temple through the city. Thousands participate in bhajans and rituals.
🥁
August – September
Ganesh Chaturthi
10-day festival with spectacular pandals, processions, and the immersion of Lord Ganesha's idol into the Godavari River at Ramkund ghat.
🕯️
October – November
Navratri at Saptashringi
Nine nights of celebrations at Saptashringi Devi Temple in Vani. Special puja, aarti, and cultural events with lakhs of devotees visiting during this period.
🍷
February
Sula Fest (SulaFest)
India's premier music and wine festival held at Sula Vineyards. Two days of live performances, wine tasting, gourmet food, and glamping in the vineyard.
🌊
Every Monday (Shravan)
Shravan Somvar
During the holy month of Shravan, thousands of devotees visit Trimbakeshwar and Someshwar for special Monday puja and abhishek to Lord Shiva.
